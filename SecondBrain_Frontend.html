Why AI / ML for this decision?
🔢 A human advisor can hold 2–3 variables in mind. This model evaluates 11 inputs × 8 paths × 4 dimensions simultaneously.
📊 The ensemble (Random Forest + Gradient Boosting + DNN + Confidence Scorer) catches non-linear interactions — e.g. burnout×runway interacts differently than burnout×network.
🔄 The what-if simulator lets users explore counterfactuals they couldn't easily test with a career counsellor in a 30-minute session.
⚡ LLM reasoning (Groq/Llama) adds qualitative framing — cognitive biases, hidden tradeoffs, reversibility — that pure ML scores miss.

⚖️ Responsibility, Ethics & Limits
What this tool is, what it isn't, and where it could go wrong
📍 Geography Bias

Trained on US BLS + UCI Census data. Salary, job availability, and path viability differ significantly in India, EU, and other markets. Do not use salary scores for non-US decisions.

🎲 Not a Prediction

Scores are probabilistic archetypes, not personal forecasts. A score of 8.1 means "profiles like yours tend toward this outcome," not "you will earn X."

🔒 No Validation

This model has not been clinically or economically validated. It has not been audited for fairness across gender, race, or socioeconomic status. Treat outputs as a thinking aid only.

🧠 LLM Hallucination

AI reasoning (Groq/Llama) may produce plausible-sounding but incorrect tradeoff analysis. Hidden risks identified by the LLM are suggestions, not facts — verify with real domain experts.

📉 Missing Signals

The model cannot capture: family dynamics, mental health nuance, systemic barriers, discrimination risk, or personal values. These often outweigh quantitative scores.

✅ Intended Use

Use this to structure your thinking and surface tradeoffs you may have missed. It replaces neither a career counsellor nor a financial advisor. The final decision is always yours.

⚠️ Confidence intervals shown are model uncertainty, not life uncertainty. Real-world outcome variance is much larger. Wider bands (Volatile) mean the model is less certain — they are not guarantees of failure.
